I. Written Questions: 1. In this dashboard we have three graphs that all give us insights about the video game sales dataset. The first graph we have is a static bar chart that represents the top 10 video games of all time according to their global sales. By having the name of the games on the y-axis and the global sales on the x-axis, it is convenient to quickly see the top 10 games of all time along with their globals sales. The second graph is a dynamic bar chart to represent the top genres by their sales in different regions. By having different buttons representing different regions, it is easy to toggle between regions and see which genre is the most popular. Finally, we have a pie chart that represents all twelve genres associated with their top publisher according to the number of sales.

2. D3 was helpful for many different reasons but here are my top the three. First of all, I found that it was very easy to customize using d3.js for animations, interactivity and data-driven plots. Second of all, I thought that the wide variety of visualization collections provided multiple ways of visualizing the data easily. Finally, d3 was helpful in that it was very flexible in making any data visualization framework while providing an extensive library.

3. One reason why d3 would not be the best tool for creating a visualization is the lack of documentation making it hard to get the hang of it. Also, I feel like more innovative visualization charts could be added. I think d3 has a steep learning curve that could benefit from more tutorials.

4. After reading “A Comprehensive Guide to Accessible Data Visualization”, I would say that my dashboard is accessible for most people. The bar graphs I created are simple enough to be understood in less than 5 seconds. This is because the graphs are not crowded with unecessary information and the labels provide a clear overview of the purpose of the graphs. Color blind people would probably have a hard time understanding the proportiong of sales for each top publisher genre in the pie chart. Putting the labels in the pie chart would have made it more accessible, however it would have made the pie chart too crowded considering there are 12 genres. The legend nonetheless clearly shows which publisher is the most popular for every genre.

5. As mentioned before, I could have made my pie chart more accessible for blind people by having the labels directly on the graph. I opted for a legend because the pie chart would have been too crowded with the labels of 12 genres. In short, it would have made the visualization harder.
